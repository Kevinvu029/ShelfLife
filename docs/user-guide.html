Chapter 1

Introduction

ShelfLife is a mobile app for iOS and Android that helps you track the expiration dates of everything in your home pantry, fridge, and freezer. By scanning a product barcode or entering an item manually, ShelfLife keeps a real-time inventory of your food — and alerts you before anything goes bad.

The average household wastes hundreds of dollars of food each year simply because items were forgotten at the back of the fridge or pantry. ShelfLife solves this by giving you a single, color-coded view of everything you own and when it expires.

Chapter 2

Getting Started

2.1 Installation

ShelfLife is available on both the Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Search for "ShelfLife — Stop Letting Food Expire" or follow the link on our listing page.

  1. Open your app store Go to the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android) on your device.
  2. Search for ShelfLife Type "ShelfLife" in the search bar and look for the app with the brown pixel-art shelf icon.
  3. Tap Install / Get Tap the Install (Android) or Get (iOS) button. The app is free with no in-app purchases.
  4. Grant camera permission When prompted, allow ShelfLife to access your camera. This is required for barcode scanning.

2.2 Creating an Account

ShelfLife uses Firebase Authentication. You can sign up with Google, Apple, or your email address.

📱 Profile Creation Screen
  1. Open ShelfLife Launch the app after installation.
  2. Tap "Get Started" or "Sign Up" You will be presented with three sign-in options: Google, Apple, and Email/Password.
  3. Choose your sign-in method Select Google for the fastest setup (uses your existing Google account), Apple (for iPhone users), or enter your email and create a password.
  4. Complete your profile On the profile creation screen, enter your display name and optionally your birthday. Tap Save.
✅ Tip: Use the Google sign-in option for the smoothest experience. It requires no password and your account syncs automatically across devices.

2.3 Signing In

If you already have an account, tap Sign In on the welcome screen and authenticate with the same method you used during registration. Your pantry data will sync automatically from the cloud.

⚠️ If you sign in with a different method than you used to register (e.g., email instead of Google), a new account will be created and your previous pantry data will not appear. Always use the same sign-in method.
Chapter 3

The Home Screen

After signing in, ShelfLife takes you to the Home Screen — your pantry dashboard.

📱 Home Screen

Key Elements

  • Expiry Summary Bar — A horizontal color bar at the top showing the proportion of items that are fresh (green), expiring soon (yellow), and expired (red) in your pantry.
  • Item Cards — Each food item is displayed as a card showing the item name, days until expiry, and a color-coded status indicator.
  • Filter Tabs — Switch between viewing All items, Expiring Soon, and Already Expired.
  • Add Button (+) — Located in the bottom right, this opens the barcode scanner to add a new item.
  • Bottom Navigation Bar — Navigate between Home, Pantry (full list), and Profile.

Reading the Expiry Bar

The colored bar at the top of the home screen gives you an instant overview of your pantry's health:

Red — Expired
Yellow — Expiring within 7 days
Green — More than 7 days remaining
✅ Tap any item card to open its full details, including the item name, expiry date, and price you paid.
Chapter 4

Adding Food Items

There are two ways to add a food item to your pantry: barcode scanning (fastest) and manual entry.

4.1 Barcode Scanner

The barcode scanner is the primary way to add items. It uses your phone's camera and the Open Food Facts database to automatically identify products.

📱 Barcode Scanner Screen
  1. Tap the + button On the Home screen, tap the floating action button (+ icon) in the bottom right corner.
  2. Point your camera at the barcode Hold your phone 4–8 inches from the barcode on the product packaging. The scanner viewfinder highlights the barcode area.
  3. Wait for recognition ShelfLife automatically scans and looks up the product. The item name and image will appear at the bottom of the screen when found.
  4. Set the expiration date Use the date picker to enter the expiry date printed on the product packaging. Tap the date field to open the calendar picker.
  5. (Optional) Enter the price Type the price you paid for the item. This is used for the price tracking and savings calculator feature.
  6. Tap Save The item is added to your pantry and appears immediately on the Home screen.
⚠️ If the barcode is not recognized, the item may not be in the Open Food Facts database. Use Manual Entry (see section 4.2) to add it by name.

Scanner Tips

  • Ensure good lighting — the scanner works best in well-lit environments.
  • Keep the camera steady for 1–2 seconds while scanning.
  • If the barcode is damaged or curved, try scanning from a slightly different angle.
  • Most standard UPC-A, EAN-13, and QR barcodes on food packaging are supported.

4.2 Manual Entry

Use manual entry when a barcode cannot be scanned or the product is not in the database.

  1. Tap the + button Open the barcode scanner screen.
  2. Tap "Enter Manually" or the keyboard icon This switches from the camera view to a text input form.
  3. Type the item name Enter a clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Whole Milk", "Greek Yogurt – Strawberry").
  4. Set the expiration date Tap the date field and use the calendar picker to select the expiry date.
  5. (Optional) Enter the price Type the price you paid for the item.
  6. Tap Save The item is saved to your pantry.
Chapter 5

Managing Your Pantry

The Pantry screen (accessible from the bottom navigation bar) shows a complete list of all items in your pantry, sorted by expiry date by default.

📱 Pantry List Screen

5.1 Expiry Status Colors

Every item in your pantry displays a color indicator that updates automatically based on today's date:

Red — Already expired
Yellow — Expires within 7 days
Green — More than 7 days remaining

The expiry date and a "days remaining" or "days ago" count are displayed beneath each item's name. For example:

  • 3 days left — shown in yellow
  • Expired 2 days ago — shown in red
  • 25 days left — shown in green

5.2 Editing an Item

You can update any item's name, expiry date, or price after it has been saved.

  1. Tap the item card In either the Home screen or Pantry list, tap the item you want to edit.
  2. Tap the Edit (pencil) icon This opens the Edit Food Details screen.
  3. Update the fields Change the item name, expiry date, or price as needed.
  4. Tap Save Your changes are saved immediately and synced to the cloud.

5.3 Deleting an Item

To remove an item from your pantry (e.g., after you've used it or thrown it away):

  1. Tap the item card Open the item's detail view.
  2. Tap the Delete (trash) icon A confirmation prompt appears: "Are you sure you want to delete this item?"
  3. Confirm deletion Tap Delete to confirm. The item is permanently removed from your pantry.
⚠️ Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. If you accidentally delete an item, you will need to re-add it using the scanner or manual entry.
Chapter 6

Price Tracking

ShelfLife lets you log the price you paid for each food item. This enables you to see exactly how much money you are saving by using food before it expires — rather than throwing it away.

Logging a Price

When adding or editing an item, fill in the Price ($) field with the amount you paid. This can be the item price, or a per-unit price if you bought in bulk.

Seeing Your Savings

The total value of food currently in your pantry is shown on the Home screen. Items that you use (delete) before they expire contribute to your saved amount. Items that expire unused represent wasted money.

💡 Check the Community Leaderboard to see how your pantry management compares to other ShelfLife users in the community.

Tips for Accurate Tracking

  • Log prices at the time of purchase while the receipt is still available.
  • For multi-packs (e.g., a 6-pack of yogurt), log each cup as a separate item with the per-cup price.
  • You can update a price at any time by editing the item.
  • Price is optional — you can track expiry without it.
Chapter 7

Account Settings

Access your account settings by tapping the Profile icon in the bottom navigation bar.

Profile Information

On the Profile screen you can view and update:

  • Display Name — The name shown on your account and (optionally) the community leaderboard.
  • Email Address — Your sign-in email. Read-only if you signed up with Google or Apple.
  • Birthday — Optional; used for personalization.
  • Profile Photo — Set via your connected Google/Apple account or uploaded directly.

Signing Out

  1. Open the Profile screen via the bottom navigation bar.
  2. Scroll to the bottom and tap Sign Out.
  3. Confirm sign out — Your pantry data remains safely in the cloud and will return when you sign back in.

Deleting Your Account

To permanently delete your account and all associated data, contact us via the GitHub repository. Account deletion removes all pantry items and personal information from our servers.

Chapter 8

Troubleshooting

Barcode not recognized

If ShelfLife cannot identify a barcode:

  • Ensure the barcode is well-lit and the camera lens is clean.
  • Move your phone closer or farther from the barcode (4–8 inches is optimal).
  • Try scanning the barcode from a slightly different angle.
  • If the product is still not found, use Manual Entry (Section 4.2).

App is not syncing

If items you added on one device are not appearing on another:

  • Check that both devices are signed in with the same account.
  • Verify your internet connection is active.
  • Pull down on the pantry list to force a refresh.
  • Sign out and sign back in to force a full sync.

Sign-in error

If you encounter an error when trying to sign in:

  • Check your internet connection.
  • Make sure you are using the same sign-in method as when you registered (Google vs. email).
  • For email sign-in, use the "Forgot password?" link to reset your password.

Camera permission denied

If the barcode scanner shows a black screen or a permissions error:

  • iOS: Go to Settings → ShelfLife → Camera and enable access.
  • Android: Go to Settings → Apps → ShelfLife → Permissions → Camera and enable access.
